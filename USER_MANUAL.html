Introduction & Overview

1.1 What is Business360?

Business360 is an all-in-one business management platform (SaaS) that brings CRM, Inventory, Accounting, HR, Projects, and Analytics together in a single place. Instead of juggling spreadsheets and disconnected apps, your entire team works in one shared system.

The platform is modular — you only activate the features your company actually needs. Start with CRM, add Accounting later, keep it lean.

🤝

CRM

Track leads, manage deals on a kanban board, and maintain your customer database.

📦

Inventory

Manage your product catalog, warehouse stock levels, and purchase orders.

💰

Accounting

Create customer invoices with line items, track expenses by category.

👥

HR

Manage employees, track leave requests, and approve time-off.

📋

Projects

Create projects, break them into tasks, assign priorities and due dates.

📊

Reports

Cross-module KPIs, monthly revenue vs. expenses, and real-time alerts.

1.2 Who this manual is for

This manual is for business users — anyone who signs up for Business360, creates or joins an organization, and uses the platform day-to-day. It covers every page, every field, and every action a regular user or organization owner can take.

ℹ️

This manual does not cover the platform administration panel (sadmin). If you are a platform administrator, refer to the separate Admin Guide.

1.3 How to read this manual

Each section follows the same structure: what the feature does, a step-by-step guide, and a reference table of all fields. Callout boxes are used throughout:

✅

Tip — a helpful shortcut or best practice.

⚠️

ℹ️

Note — extra context or clarification.

Getting Started

2.1 Creating your account

Visit /register to create your personal Business360 account.

  1. 1
    Open the Business360 website and click Get Started or navigate directly to /register.
  2. 2
    Enter your Full Name, Email Address, and choose a Password.
  3. 3
    Click Create Account. You will be redirected to the login page.
FieldNotes
Full NameYour display name shown to teammates.
EmailUsed to log in. Must be unique on the platform.
PasswordChoose a strong password. You can change it later in Settings.
⚠️

If registration fails with "email already in use," that email already has an account. Use Forgot Password to regain access instead.

2.2 Logging in

Visit /login to sign in to an existing account.

  1. 1
    Enter your Email and Password.
  2. 2
    Click Sign In.
  3. 3
    If this is your first login, you will be redirected to the Onboarding flow to set up your organization. Otherwise, you go directly to your Dashboard.
ℹ️

For security, there is a rate limit of 20 login attempts per 15 minutes per device. If you are locked out, wait 15 minutes and try again.

2.3 Forgot your password?

  1. 1
    On the login page, click Forgot password? to go to /forgot-password.
  2. 2
    Enter the email address linked to your account and click Send Reset Link.
  3. 3
    Check your inbox for a password-reset email. The link is valid for 1 hour.
  4. 4
    Click the link in the email. You will land on /reset-password.
  5. 5
    Enter and confirm your new password, then click Reset Password.
  6. 6
    You will be redirected to the login page. Log in with your new password.
✅

The reset form always shows a success message regardless of whether the email exists — this is intentional, to protect account privacy.

2.4 Setting up your organization (Onboarding)

After your first login you will be taken through a 3-step onboarding wizard at /onboarding. This creates your organization — the private workspace your whole team will share.

  1. 1
    Name your organization. Enter the company or team name that will be shown in your workspace.
  2. 2
    Choose a subscription plan. Compare the available plans (Starter, Growth, Pro, Enterprise). You can upgrade at any time later.
  3. 3
    Confirm & create. Review your choices and click Create Organization. Your private workspace is provisioned immediately.
✅

Most plans include a free trial period. You can explore the full platform during this time without entering payment details.

Understanding the Dashboard

3.1 The main dashboard screen

After logging in, your home screen is the Dashboard (/dashboard). It gives you an instant health-check of your entire business:

  • KPI cards — animated counters showing total revenue, open deals, active employees, and more. Each card shows a trend arrow (↑↓) comparing to last month.
  • Live stats strip — compact row of real-time numbers pulled from every installed module.
  • Module grid — tiles for each installed module. Click any tile to jump directly to that module.
  • Trial banner — shown when your organization is in trial mode, with the days remaining and an upgrade link.
ℹ️

The KPI cards only appear when at least one module is installed. Before installing any modules, the dashboard shows an empty state with a prompt to visit the App Store.

3.2 Sidebar navigation

The left sidebar is your main navigation. It shows:

ItemGoes to
DashboardYour home screen overview
App StoreBrowse and install modules
My ModulesManage your installed modules
Installed module linksCRM, Inventory, Accounting, HR, Projects, Reports — only shown if installed
SettingsAccount, password, org, and team management

On mobile, the sidebar is hidden by default. Tap the menu icon in the header to reveal it.

3.3 Header bar: alerts, dark mode, profile

The top bar on every dashboard page contains:

ElementWhat it does
BreadcrumbShows your current location (e.g. Dashboard › CRM › Leads)
🌙 / ☀️ Dark mode toggleSwitches between light and dark theme. Your preference is saved and remembered across sessions.
🔔 Alerts bellShows a red badge if there are overdue invoices or low-stock items. Click to see a grouped list.
User avatarShows your initials. Clicking it allows you to log out.

3.4 Command palette (Ctrl+K)

Press Ctrl+K (or ⌘+K on Mac) anywhere in the dashboard to open the Command Palette — a quick-search overlay that lets you jump to any page instantly.

  • Start typing to filter pages by name (e.g. type "lead" to find CRM Leads).
  • Use ↑ / ↓ arrow keys to highlight a result.
  • Press Enter to navigate, or Esc to close.
  • Pages are grouped by section: Navigation, CRM, Inventory, Accounting, HR, Projects.

3.5 Trial banner

If your organization is in a trial period, a green banner appears at the top of the dashboard showing the number of trial days remaining and a direct link to upgrade your plan. Once the trial ends, contact your platform administrator to activate a paid plan.

4

Your Account & Organization Settings

Navigate to Settings from the sidebar (/dashboard/settings) to manage your personal account, your password, your organization's details, and team members.

4.1 Account information

The Account section shows your current name, email address, and your role within the organization. This is read-only — to change your name or email, contact your organization owner.

4.2 Changing your password

  1. 1
    Scroll to the Change Password section.
  2. 2
    Enter your Current Password to verify your identity.
  3. 3
    Enter and confirm your New Password.
  4. 4
    Click Update Password. A success toast will confirm the change.
⚠️

If you enter the wrong current password, the update will be rejected. Use Forgot Password from the login page if you have forgotten it.

4.3 Organization information

The Organization section shows your organization's name, current subscription plan, and account status. Only owners can edit organization details.

4.4 Managing team members

The Members section lists everyone in your organization with their name, email, and role. Owners can invite new members and change member roles.

ℹ️

The number of members you can add depends on your subscription plan. Check your plan's member limit under Organization details.

4.5 Roles & permissions

Business360 has three roles within an organization:

👑 Owner

Full control

  • All manager permissions
  • Delete the organization
  • Billing authority
  • Promote/demote members

🛠 Manager

Team management

  • All member permissions
  • Install/uninstall modules
  • Manage settings
  • Invite team members

👤 Member

Standard access

  • Use installed modules
  • Create/edit/delete records
  • View reports
  • Export CSV data
5

App Store & Modules

5.1 Browsing the App Store

Click App Store in the sidebar to reach /dashboard/store. All available modules are shown as cards. Each card displays:

  • Module name and icon
  • Category (e.g. Sales, Finance, Operations)
  • Star rating
  • Short description
  • Whether the module is already installed

Click any card to see the full module detail page, including a longer description, version history, and screenshots.

Below the main grid, a Community Marketplace section shows third-party modules submitted by independent developers. Each marketplace card shows the author name, a link to the source repository, and the price (if paid).

5.2 Installing a module

  1. 1
    In the sidebar, click My Modules (/dashboard/modules).
  2. 2
    Find the module you want and click Install.
  3. 3
    The module is activated instantly. A new link appears in the sidebar.
⚠️

You must be an Owner or Manager to install modules. Members cannot install or uninstall modules.

5.3 Uninstalling a module

  1. 1
    Go to My Modules.
  2. 2
    Find the installed module and click Uninstall.
  3. 3
    Confirm the action in the dialog box.
⚠️

Uninstalling a module hides its pages from the sidebar, but your existing data (leads, invoices, etc.) is not deleted. If you reinstall the module, your data will be there.

5.4 Community Marketplace

The Community Marketplace section of the App Store shows modules built by third-party developers and approved by the Business360 platform team. These modules extend the platform with specialized features. Contact the developer via the listed repository link for support on community modules.

CRM Module

The CRM (Customer Relationship Management) module helps you track your sales pipeline from the first contact all the way to a paying customer. Access it via the sidebar at /dashboard/crm.

The CRM overview page shows summary KPIs: total leads, open deals, pipeline value, and customer count.

6.1 Leads

Leads are potential customers who have shown interest but haven't yet committed. Manage them at /dashboard/crm/leads.

Adding a lead

  1. 1
    Click Add Lead (top-right of the leads page).
  2. 2
    Fill in the lead details in the slide-in panel.
  3. 3
    Click Save. The lead appears in the table.

Lead fields

FieldDescription
NameContact's full name. Required.
EmailContact email address.
PhoneContact phone number.
CompanyThe company they work for.
Statusnew contacted qualified converted lost
SourceWhere the lead came from (website, referral, event, etc.).
NotesAny additional context about this lead.

Filtering and pagination

Use the Status filter dropdown at the top of the table to show only leads in a specific status. Results are paginated (20 per page) — use the Previous / Next buttons at the bottom.

Editing and deleting

Click the edit icon (pencil) on any lead row to open the edit panel. Click the delete icon (trash) and confirm the dialog to delete a lead permanently.

6.2 Deals (Kanban board)

Deals represent sales opportunities with a monetary value. They are managed on a drag-and-drop kanban board at /dashboard/crm/deals.

Kanban columns

ColumnMeaning
ProspectEarly stage — exploring the opportunity
ProposalProposal sent or in discussion
NegotiationActively negotiating terms
Closed WonDeal successfully closed
Closed LostDeal did not go through

Moving deals

Drag any deal card and drop it into a different column. The status updates automatically and is saved immediately. If the save fails, the card returns to its original column.

Deal fields

FieldDescription
TitleName of the deal. Required.
ValueExpected monetary value of the deal.
CompanyCompany associated with this deal.
ContactPerson you are dealing with.
StatusCurrent pipeline stage (set by dragging, or from the form).
Close DateExpected date the deal will close.

6.3 Customers

Customers are people or companies you have already done business with. Manage them at /dashboard/crm/customers.

FieldDescription
NameCustomer's full name. Required.
EmailPrimary email address.
PhoneContact phone number.
CompanyCompany name.
NotesAdditional background or preferences.

6.4 Converting a lead to a customer

When a lead is ready to become a customer, use the one-click Convert action — no need to re-enter details manually.

  1. 1
    Go to /dashboard/crm/leads.
  2. 2
    Find the lead and click the green convert icon (person with a plus sign) on that row.
  3. 3
    Confirm the action. A new Customer record is created automatically from the lead's name, email, phone, and company.
  4. 4
    The lead's status changes to converted and the convert button is hidden for that row.
✅

Converted leads remain visible in the Leads table for reference. You can filter by status to hide them from your active pipeline view.

Inventory Module

The Inventory module tracks your product catalog, warehouse locations, stock quantities, and purchase orders. Access it at /dashboard/inventory.

The overview page shows KPI cards: product count, warehouse count, purchase order count, and products-by-status breakdown.

7.1 Products

Products are the items your business buys, sells, or stores. Manage them at /dashboard/inventory/products.

FieldDescription
NameProduct display name. Required.
SKUStock-keeping unit — your internal product code.
CategoryProduct category for grouping and filtering.
Unit PricePrice you sell this product for.
Cost PricePrice you paid to acquire or produce this product.
UnitUnit of measurement (e.g. pcs, kg, litre).
Statusactive inactive discontinued

Use the search bar at the top to filter by name, SKU, or category. Results are paginated (20 per page).

7.2 Stock levels

Each product can have stock levels tracked across multiple warehouses. Click the stock icon on any product row to open the Stock Levels dialog.

  • The dialog lists every warehouse and shows the current Quantity and Minimum Quantity (the level that triggers a low-stock alert).
  • Edit the quantity and minimum quantity fields and click Save.
  • Rows with quantity at or below the minimum are highlighted in amber to signal low stock.
ℹ️

Low-stock items also appear in the Alerts bell in the header bar so you never miss a restock.

7.3 Warehouses

Warehouses are physical storage locations. Manage them at /dashboard/inventory/warehouses. They are shown as cards, each displaying the warehouse name, location, and how many products are stocked there.

FieldDescription
NameWarehouse name or identifier. Required.
LocationPhysical address or description of where it is.
DescriptionOptional notes about this warehouse.

7.4 Purchase Orders

Purchase Orders (POs) track items you are buying from suppliers. Manage them at /dashboard/inventory/orders.

FieldDescription
SupplierName of the supplier or vendor. Required.
Statusdraft ordered received cancelled
Order DateDate the order was placed.
Expected DateDate you expect delivery.
Total AmountAuto-calculated from line items.
Line ItemsIndividual products ordered: product name, quantity, unit cost, and line total.

Adding line items to a purchase order

  1. 1
    Create or edit a purchase order to open the edit panel.
  2. 2
    In the Line Items table, click Add Item.
  3. 3
    Enter the product name, quantity, and unit cost. The line total is calculated automatically.
  4. 4
    Add as many items as needed. Click the × on a row to remove it.
  5. 5
    Click Save. The order total updates automatically.

Use the search bar to filter orders by supplier name. You can also export the orders list to CSV.

Accounting Module

The Accounting module manages your invoices and business expenses. Access it at /dashboard/accounting.

The overview page shows KPI cards: total paid revenue, outstanding (unpaid) amount, total expenses, and invoice count by status.

8.1 Invoices & line items

Invoices are bills you send to customers. Manage them at /dashboard/accounting/invoices.

FieldDescription
Invoice NumberYour unique invoice reference (e.g. INV-001). Required.
CustomerName of the customer being billed. Required.
Statusdraft sent paid overdue cancelled
Issue DateDate the invoice was created (defaults to today).
Due DatePayment deadline date.
SubtotalAuto-calculated from line items.
TaxTax amount entered manually.
TotalSubtotal + Tax (calculated automatically).
Line ItemsIndividual charges: description, quantity, unit price, and line total.

Adding line items to an invoice

  1. 1
    Create or edit an invoice to open the edit panel.
  2. 2
    In the Line Items table, click Add Item.
  3. 3
    Enter the description, quantity, and unit price. The line total and subtotal recalculate automatically.
  4. 4
    Enter the Tax amount (this is a separate manual field).
  5. 5
    Click Save.
ℹ️

Invoices with status overdue (due date in the past and not paid or cancelled) appear in the Alerts bell in the header.

Use the search bar to filter by invoice number or customer name. Results are paginated (20 per page). Export to CSV using the Export CSV button.

8.2 Expenses

Expenses track money your business has spent. Manage them at /dashboard/accounting/expenses.

FieldDescription

ReferenceReceipt number, invoice reference, or any tracking code.

Results are paginated (20 per page). Export to CSV using the Export CSV button.

Reports Module

The Reports module brings together data from every other installed module in one place, giving you a complete picture of your business at a glance. Access it at /dashboard/reports.

11.1 KPI overview

The top of the Reports page shows four headline KPI cards:

CardWhat it shows
Paid RevenueTotal from all paid invoices
Total ExpensesSum of all recorded expenses
Net ProfitPaid Revenue − Total Expenses
Pipeline ValueTotal value of all open deals in CRM

Below the headline cards, a Module Summary grid shows compact numbers from each module:

  • CRM: leads count, customers count
  • HR: total employees
  • Inventory: product count
  • Accounting: invoice count
  • Projects: task count

11.2 Monthly P&L table

Below the KPIs, a Monthly Profit & Loss table shows the last 12 months with columns for:

  • Revenue — total from paid invoices in that month
  • Expenses — total expenses recorded in that month
  • Profit — Revenue − Expenses (green if positive, red if negative)

A bar chart visually compares revenue (green) and expenses (red) side-by-side for each month, with hover tooltips showing the exact figures.

11.3 Alerts bell

The Alerts bell 🔔 in the top header bar shows a badge count when there are items needing attention. Clicking it opens a grouped dropdown:

  • Overdue Invoices — invoices whose due date has passed and are not yet paid or cancelled.
  • Low Stock Items — products in any warehouse where the quantity is at or below the minimum quantity threshold.

Click any alert item to navigate directly to the relevant page (Invoices or Products) to take action.

Exporting Data (CSV)

You can export your data as a CSV file (spreadsheet) from most table pages. This is useful for backup, offline analysis, or sharing data with external tools.

PageWhat is exported
CRM — LeadsAll lead records (filtered view)
CRM — CustomersAll customer records
Inventory — ProductsFull product catalog
Inventory — Purchase OrdersAll purchase orders
Accounting — InvoicesAll invoice headers (without line items)
Accounting — ExpensesAll expense records
HR — EmployeesAll employee records
HR — Leave RequestsAll leave request records
Projects — TasksAll task records

How to export

  1. 1
    Navigate to the table page you want to export.
  2. 2
    Click the Export CSV button (usually top-right of the table).
  3. 3
    Your browser downloads a .csv file automatically. Open it in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app.
⚠️

CSV exports use the current page's data. If you have filtered the table, only filtered results are exported. To export everything, clear all filters first.

Tips, Shortcuts & Best Practices

Keyboard shortcuts

ShortcutAction

General tips

  • Dark mode — click the Sun/Moon icon in the header. Your preference is remembered permanently.
  • Alerts bell — check it regularly. Overdue invoices and low-stock alerts appear there before they become bigger problems.
  • Pagination — tables load 20 records per page by default. Use the page controls at the bottom to navigate, or use the search bar to narrow down results.
  • Confirm dialogs — all delete actions require a confirmation click. This prevents accidental data loss.
  • Toast notifications — a small notification appears in the corner of the screen to confirm every successful save or delete action. If you see an error toast, read the message — it usually explains what went wrong.
  • Kanban drag-and-drop — on the Deals page, you can drag cards to new columns using a mouse or touch. The highlighted ring on a column shows where the card will land.

Best practices by module

ModuleRecommendation
CRMConvert leads to customers as soon as a deal closes, to keep your lead list focused on active prospects.
InventorySet minimum quantities for all products so the alerts bell warns you before you run out of stock.
AccountingUpdate invoice statuses promptly — "overdue" invoices show in alerts so you know who to follow up with.
HRProcess leave requests promptly. Pending requests are easy to miss if you have a long list.
ProjectsAssign every task to a specific person and set a due date — this makes the Reports module more meaningful.
ReportsCheck the monthly P&L table at the end of each month to spot expense trends early.

Glossary

TermDefinition
OrganizationYour company's private workspace within Business360. All your data, team members, and installed modules belong to one organization.
ModuleAn optional feature pack (CRM, Inventory, etc.) that you install or uninstall as needed.
TenantTechnical term for an organization in a multi-tenant system — each organization is isolated from all others.
OwnerThe highest role in an organization. Has full control including billing and member management.
ManagerA team lead role. Can manage modules, settings, and team members, but cannot delete the organization.
MemberStandard user role. Can use all installed modules but cannot change settings or install modules.
KPIKey Performance Indicator — a headline metric (e.g. total revenue, employee count) shown on overview pages.
SaaSSoftware as a Service — software you access through a web browser, hosted centrally, without installing anything.
CSVComma-Separated Values — a plain-text file format for tabular data, readable by Excel and Google Sheets.
KanbanA visual board where items (deals) are represented as cards that move across status columns.
SKUStock Keeping Unit — a unique internal code that identifies a product.
P&LProfit & Loss — a financial summary comparing revenue to expenses.
JWTJSON Web Token — the encrypted login ticket your browser receives after signing in. It is stored securely and sent automatically with every request.
Trial periodA limited-time free access window during which you can use all features without paying.
Command PaletteA keyboard-driven search overlay (Ctrl+K) for jumping to any page in the dashboard instantly.
MarketplaceThe section of the App Store that lists third-party modules built by independent developers.
