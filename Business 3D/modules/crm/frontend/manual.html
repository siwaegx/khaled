SECTION 1

Login & Getting Started

How to Log In

Business 3D uses a 4-digit PIN — no username or password to remember.

Open your browser and go to http://192.168.8.6:3000 (or the address your manager gave you).
You will see a PIN pad. Type your 4-digit PIN using the buttons or your keyboard.
Press the → (Enter) button or press Enter on your keyboard.
The Dashboard loads automatically. You are now in!

Login Screen Screenshot

Your PIN is given to you by your manager. If you forget it, ask your manager to reset it — only managers can change PINs.

Installing as a Mobile App

You can install Business 3D on your phone for quick access — no App Store needed.

DeviceSteps
AndroidOpen Chrome → go to the CRM URL → tap the 3-dot menu ⋮ → "Add to Home screen" → tap Install
iPhone / iPadOpen Safari → go to the CRM URL → tap the Share button ⬆ → "Add to Home Screen" → tap Add
On iPhone you must use Safari, not Chrome, for the install option to appear.

Logging Out

Click the ⏻ logout icon at the bottom-left of the sidebar. Your session will end and the PIN screen will appear again.

SECTION 3

Contacts

A Contact is an individual person you deal with — a purchasing manager, engineer, owner, etc. Each contact can be linked to a company.

Adding a New Contact

Click Contacts in the left sidebar.
Click the blue + New Contact button (top-right).
Fill in the form. Required: First Name and Last Name.
Set the Lead Status to describe where this contact is in your sales process.
Click Save. The contact appears in your list immediately.

Lead Statuses Explained

Lead status tells you and your manager the temperature of each contact:

🔵 Fresh Lead — Brand new, not yet contacted

🔥 Hot — Very interested, follow up immediately

❄️ Cold — Not interested right now, revisit later

⭐ VIP — Key customer, treat with high priority

📋 RFQ — Sent a Request For Quotation, waiting for response

🏁 Need Visit — Requires an in-person visit

✅ Done Sales — Sale completed

🚫 Not Interested — Declined, archive this lead

📝 Registered — Formally registered as a customer
Update the lead status after every interaction. This is the most important habit for keeping your pipeline accurate.

Quick Actions on a Contact Card

Each contact card in the list has quick-action buttons:

ButtonAction
📞 Phone iconInstantly log a phone call for this contact
📍 Pin iconInstantly log a visit
📝 Note iconAdd a quick note
💬 WhatsApp iconOpen WhatsApp chat with this contact's number

Viewing Contact Details

Click any contact card to open the detail panel on the right.
You can see their full info, linked company, all past activities, and deals.
Click the pencil ✏️ Edit button to update their information.

Searching & Filtering Contacts

  • Use the Search box at the top of the Contacts page to find by name, phone, or email.
  • Use the Status filter dropdown to show only contacts with a specific lead status.

Companies

A Company is a business account you work with. Companies can have multiple contacts, deals, and activities linked to them.

Adding a New Company

Click Companies in the left sidebar.
Click + New Company (top-right).
Enter the company name (required) and as many details as you know.

Before adding a company, use the global search (🔍 top bar) to check it doesn't already exist — duplicates waste time.

Every time you move a deal to a new stage, the system automatically logs a stage change activity — no need to add a note manually.

Adding a Deal

Click Deals in the sidebar to see the Kanban board.
Click + New Deal.
Fill in Title, Value (in EGP), Stage, and link to a Company and/or Contact.
Set a Close Date — your target date to close the deal.
Click Save.

Moving a Deal (Drag & Drop)

On the Kanban view, click and hold a deal card.
Drag it to the new stage column and release.
The stage updates and a stage-change activity is logged automatically.
Switch to List view (the ≡ icon) when you have many deals — it's easier to sort and see all details at once.

Probability

Each deal has a probability % (0–100). Use this to estimate how likely the deal is to close. The Dashboard uses this to calculate your Forecast Value (pipeline × probability).

SECTION 6

Activities — Logging Your Work

Activities are the record of everything you do: calls made, visits done, emails sent, meetings held. Logging activities is essential — it shows your manager your work and keeps the account history complete.

Tasks Page Screenshot

Activity Types

TypeWhen to use
📞 CallAny phone call you made or received
📍 VisitAn in-person visit to a company or contact
✉️ EmailAn email you sent or received
🤝 MeetingA formal meeting (at your office or theirs)
✅ TaskA to-do item — for yourself or assigned to a colleague
📝 NoteA general note about a contact or company

How to Log a Call or Visit (Quick Method)

The fastest way — directly from the contact card:

Go to Contacts and find the contact.
On their card, click the 📞 phone button (for call) or 📍 pin button (for visit).
A quick form appears. Add a short title (e.g. "Discussed pricing") and optional note.
Click Save. The activity is logged instantly.

How to Log a Full Activity

Click Activities in the sidebar (or open a contact/company detail and click + Log Activity).
Click + New Activity.
Choose the Type, write a Title, and add a Description with details.
Set a Due Date if it's a scheduled activity (meeting, planned visit).
Link it to a Contact and/or Company.
Click Save.

Completing an Activity

When you finish a task or scheduled activity, mark it as done:

  • Open the Activities page.
  • Click the ✓ checkmark next to the activity.
  • It moves to the "Completed" section.
Never skip logging a visit or call — even a 2-minute call should be logged. This history is what makes the CRM valuable over time.

SECTION 7

Tasks & Reminders

Tasks

Tasks are to-do items with a due date. Use them to track follow-ups, send quotations, make a call, etc.

Creating a Task

Click Tasks in the sidebar.
Click + New Task.
Write the task title (e.g. "Follow up with Ahmed about the quotation").
Set a Due Date so it shows on your calendar.
Link it to a Contact and/or Company if relevant.
Click Save.
Check your Tasks page every morning. Complete tasks that are done by clicking the ✓ button.

Overdue Tasks

The Dashboard shows an overdue count in red. If you have overdue tasks, complete them or update their due date — your manager can see this number.

Reminders

When creating an activity, you can set a Reminder Date/Time. The system will send you a notification when the reminder fires.

  • Reminders appear in the 🔔 bell icon at the top of the screen.
  • Click the bell to see all your unread notifications.
  • The Reminders page in the sidebar shows all activities that have a reminder set.

SECTION 8

Calendar

The Calendar shows all activities that have a due date on a monthly grid — visits, meetings, tasks, calls scheduled for a specific day.

Using the Calendar

  • Click Calendar in the sidebar.
  • Use the < > arrows to move between months.
  • Click the Today button to jump back to the current month.
  • Click any event on the calendar to see its details.
  • Different activity types are shown in different colors so you can tell calls, visits, and meetings apart at a glance.
Schedule your visits and meetings by creating activities with due dates — they will appear on your calendar automatically. No separate scheduling is needed.

SECTION 9

Global Search

The search bar at the top of every page lets you find anything instantly across contacts, companies, and deals.

How to Search

Click the search box in the top bar (or press / on your keyboard).
Type at least 2 characters — name, phone number, city, or company name.
Results appear instantly in a dropdown, grouped by Contacts, Companies, and Deals.
Click any result to go directly to that record.
Search by phone number to quickly check if a contact already exists before adding a new one — this prevents duplicates.

SECTION 11

Tips & Best Practices

Daily Habits

  1. Morning: Open the Dashboard — check upcoming activities and overdue tasks.
  2. During the day: Log every call and visit immediately after it happens — don't wait until the end of the day.
  3. After a meeting: Update the deal stage and add a note about what was discussed.
  4. End of day: Create tasks for tomorrow's follow-ups so you start the next day prepared.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

MistakeWhy it's a problemWhat to do instead
Not logging activitiesNo history = no proof of work; loses context on handoverLog every call, visit, email — even a 1-minute call
Not updating lead statusManager sees inaccurate pipelineUpdate status after every contact interaction
Adding duplicate contactsConfusion, split historyAlways search first before adding
Leaving overdue tasksMissed follow-ups, manager flags itComplete or reschedule tasks daily
Empty notesNo context when you revisit in 2 weeksWrite what was said, what was promised, next step

What a Good Activity Note Looks Like

✅ Good note example
Called Ahmed Hassan (Purchasing MNG at Nile Corp). He confirmed they need a water treatment system for their new Badr City plant. Budget is around 500K EGP. He asked for a quotation by next Thursday. Follow-up call scheduled.
❌ Bad note example
Called. Will call again.
